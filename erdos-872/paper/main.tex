\author{Draft for internal circulation}
\date{April 20, 2026}

\begin{document}

\begin{abstract}
We study the saturation game on the divisibility poset of $\{2,3,\dots,n\}$.
Two players alternately choose integers subject to the condition that the
chosen set remains primitive.  We assume that Prolonger moves first and seeks to
maximize the number of turns, while Shortener seeks to minimize it.  We prove a
structural reduction from terminal game positions to a weighted lower-half
optimization problem, establish lower bounds of order
$n\log\log n/\log n$ and $n(\log\log n)^2/\log n$, determine the exact first-hit
cover constant $5/24$, and prove the upper bound
\[
  \L(n) \le \left(\frac{\Wfour}{2}+o(1)\right)n
  = (0.1897112+o(1))n < 0.19n.
\]
We also prove positive results for two restricted carrier classes and develop a
taxonomy of proof-class barriers explaining why sharper upper bounds are
structurally difficult.
\end{abstract}
