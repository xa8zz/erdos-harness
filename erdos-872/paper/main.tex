\begin{abstract}
We study the saturation game on the divisibility poset of
$\{2,3,\ldots,n\}$.  Two players alternately choose integers subject to the
condition that the chosen set remains primitive; Prolonger moves first and
maximizes the final number of moves, while Shortener minimizes it.  We prove
\[
  \L(n)\le \left(\frac{\Wfour}{2}+o(1)\right)n
  =(0.1897112+o(1))n<0.19n,
\]
where $\Wfour$ is an explicit fourth-order Bonferroni constant coming from the
local logarithmic density of Shortener's legal-prime prefix.  We also prove a
polynomial lower bound for the weighted shield functional, an exact
$5n/24+O(1)$ first-hit cover theorem for the lower half, and the intermediate
upper bounds $13n/36+o(n)$ and $5n/16+o(n)$.  A conditional lower bound of
order $n(\log\log n)^2/\log n$ is included under a restricted finite safe-edge
hypothesis which is stated explicitly.  The paper also records three
proof-class barriers for local relaxations, Johnson-shadow separators, and
separator-only carrier strategies.
\end{abstract}
