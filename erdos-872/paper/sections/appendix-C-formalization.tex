\subsection{Interpretation}

The formalization status is strongest for the static reductions and the
intermediate $13/36$ strategy theorem.  The remaining formal gaps are of two
types only:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item classical analytic inputs not yet exposed conveniently in the pinned
  Mathlib snapshot;
  \item wrappers translating a formally verified finite core into the exact
  asymptotic theorem stated in the paper.
\end{enumerate}

This is why the paper treats \Cref{thm:shield-reduction,thm:1336}
and the Round~15 endgame reduction as fully formalized, treats the structural
core of \Cref{thm:524-cover} as fully formalized with an elementary
cardinality post-processing step, and presents
\Cref{thm:theorem-a,thm:516,thm:t2,thm:main-upper}
with more precise caveats.
