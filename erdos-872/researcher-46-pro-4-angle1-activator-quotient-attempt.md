The proposed activator-quotient state inequality attempts to resolve the certificate-packing obstruction by collapsing the weight of all shields first-hit by a Prolonger facet $F$ into a single activator count, $Q_h$. While this conceptually targets the geometric multiplicity of a standard local burst-star, it is mathematically shattered by the full topological capacity of a facet. ### 1. The Explicit Construction: The Global Burst-Star
Let $h \sim \log\log n$ be the central rank. Prolonger targets primes in a single dyadic interval $(Y, 2Y]$ with $Y \asymp h$.

1. **Target Selection:** Prolonger selects a single full facet $F = \{x_1, \dots, x_h\} \in \Omega_h$.
2. **Shared Local Packet:** Prolonger designates a strictly disjoint local packet $P \subset (Y, 2Y]$ (so $P \cap F = \varnothing$) of size $M = \lfloor \log h \rfloor$. 
3. **The Global Simplex of Shields:** For *every* pair $\{x_i, x_j\} \subset F$, Prolonger designates the defect-2 shield $D_{i,j} = F \setminus \{x_i, x_j\}$. There are exactly $\binom{h}{2}$ such shields natively contained within $F$.
4. **Locally Useful Completions:** For each shield $D_{i,j}$, Prolonger maintains a valid fraction $\theta \asymp 1/\log h$ of Cartesian completions by pairing the shield with leaves from $P$. A completion takes the form $E = D_{i,j} \cup \{y_a, y_b\}$, where $\{y_a, y_b\} \subset P$. 
5. **Activation:** Prolonger plays the single move $F$.

### 2. The Failure Mechanism

**Simultaneous First-Hit & Strict Distinctness:**
Because $D_{i,j} \subset F$ for all $\binom{h}{2}$ pairs, playing the single facet $F$ simultaneously first-hits the entire simplex of shields. Consequently, $F$ acts as an activator and is counted exactly once in the quotient measure: $Q_h$ increases by exactly $w(F)$.

Crucially, because the packet $P$ is disjoint from the core $F$, every generated completion is strictly distinct. If two completions were identical:
$$ F \setminus \{x_i, x_j\} \cup \{y_a, y_b\} = F \setminus \{x_k, x_l\} \cup \{y_c, y_d\} $$
Intersecting both sides with $P$ uniquely identifies the leaf pair ($\{y_a, y_b\} = \{y_c, y_d\}$), which in turn uniquely identifies the dropped core pair ($\{x_i, x_j\} = \{x_k, x_l\}$). 
Thus, Prolonger natively generates $N_{\text{ext}} = \binom{h}{2} \theta \binom{M}{2}$ mathematically distinct rank-$h$ elements. 

**Forced $\sigma^\star$ Response:**
When $F$ is played, Shortener's $\sigma^\star$ strategy must respond by claiming the legal divisor with the maximum unresolved harmonic degree. 
- A core divisor, such as $x_1 \in F$, divides any completion $E$ as long as $x_1$ was not one of the two removed elements (i.e., $1 \notin \{i, j\}$). This occurs for $\binom{h-1}{2}$ out of the $\binom{h}{2}$ shields. Thus, $x_1$ divides a $(1 - 2/h) \to 100\%$ fraction of all $N_{\text{ext}}$ generated completions.
- A leaf divisor $y_1 \in P$ only divides completions containing it, covering a $\frac{M-1}{\binom{M}{2}} = 2/M \to 0\%$ fraction of the completions.

Because $1 - 2/h \gg 2/M$, $\sigma^\star$ is rigidly forced to claim a core divisor. In doing so, Shortener irreversibly kills $\approx 100\%$ of the $N_{\text{ext}}$ distinct completions, dumping them simultaneously into the closure $\operatorname{Cl}_h$.

### 3. The Mathematical Gap (Inequality Violation)

Let us evaluate the true closure measure generated. The raw harmonic weight of each generated completion $E$ is bounded below because all primes originate from the same dyadic window $(Y, 2Y]$:
$$ \mu(E) = \mu(F) \cdot \frac{x_i x_j}{y_a y_b} = w(F) \cdot \frac{x_i x_j}{y_a y_b} \ge \frac{1}{4} w(F) $$

The total forced actual closure mass generated by this single Prolonger move is:
$$ \mu(\operatorname{Cl}_h) \ge \frac{1}{4} \binom{h}{2} \theta \binom{M}{2} w(F) \asymp h^2 \frac{1}{\log h} (\log h)^2 w(F) = h^2 \log h \cdot w(F) $$

We now evaluate Angle 1's conjectured state inequality. Because $F$ successfully absorbed the multiplicity in the quotient metric, $Q_h = w(F)$. Angle 1 claims:
$$ \text{RHS Bound} = \frac{h}{\log h} Q_h = \frac{h}{\log h} w(F) $$

Comparing the explicitly forced closure mass (LHS) to Angle 1's bound (RHS):
$$ \frac{\text{LHS}}{\text{RHS}} \asymp \frac{h^2 \log h \cdot w(F)}{\frac{h}{\log h} w(F)} = h \log^2 h $$
Because the central rank $h \sim \log\log n \to \infty$, the true required closure mass violently exceeds the proposed bound by a diverging geometric factor of $h \log^2 h$.
