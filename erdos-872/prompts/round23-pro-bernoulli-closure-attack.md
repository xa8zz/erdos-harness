A specific combinatorial-number-theoretic gap is isolated below. The question has been reduced to a clean probabilistic statement about Bernoulli measures on squarefree divisor complexes. Prove it or refute it.

## Problem

Two-player combinatorial game on $\{2, 3, \ldots, n\}$:

- Players (Prolonger and Shortener) alternate choosing integers into a shared set $A$.
- $A$ must remain an antichain under divisibility at all times.
- The game ends when $A$ is a maximal antichain (no legal move remains).
- Prolonger moves first and maximizes total moves; Shortener minimizes.
- $L(n)$ denotes the total number of moves under optimal play.

## Established background

Rigorous bounds:
- $L(n) \ge (1/8 + o(1)) \cdot n \log\log n / \log n$ (T1).
- $L(n) \ge c_\delta \cdot n(\log\log n)^2/\log n$ (T2; Maker-first weighted pair-capture + scored 3-uniform hypergraph capture + activation-stage tokenized potential).
- $L(n) \le 0.18969\, n$ (refined Chebyshev with piecewise log-scale density).

Linear lower-bound routes (single-rank fan, per-rank iteration, uniform multi-rank shielding, dyadic-fiber positive density) are all closed — dyadic fibers via an exact reduction showing single-fiber positive density $\iff$ the original linear conjecture, rescaled.

## The specific gap

The matching upper bound $L(n) = O(n (\log\log n)^k/\log n)$ for some $k \ge 2$, or any $o(n)$ rate, has been reduced to the following question.

Let $y = \exp((\log n)^{1/2})$. Let $C_y = \{d \le n/2 : d \text{ squarefree}, P^+(d) \le y\}$. The reduction theorem
$$L(n) \le 2|C_y| + 1 + \sup_{\text{reachable } (P, S)} w\!\left(R_{C_y}(P, S)\right)$$
is rigorous, where $R_{C_y}$ is the residual legal-move set after Shortener sweeps $C_y$. Since $|C_y| \le \Psi(n, y) = o(n)$, proving $\sup_{\text{reachable}} w(R_{C_y}) = o(n)$ gives $L(n) = o(n)$.

Let $\mathcal{K}(P) \subseteq C_y$ be the down-closed complex generated by Prolonger's sweep moves:
$$\mathcal{K}(P) := \{d \in C_y : d \mid p\ \text{for some Prolonger sweep move}\ p\}.$$

A sufficient bound on residual width is
$$\sup_{\text{reachable } P} \sum_{d \in \mathcal{K}(P)} \frac{1}{\varphi(d)} = o(\log y).$$

## The probabilistic reformulation (the version you are asked to attack)

Let $D_y$ be the random squarefree product formed by including each prime $p \le y$ independently with probability $1/p$. Let $Z_y = \prod_{p \le y} p/(p-1) = e^\gamma \log y + O(1)$.

For every squarefree $y$-smooth $d$:
$$\Pr(D_y = d) = \frac{1}{Z_y \varphi(d)}.$$

Consequently, for every squarefree $y$-smooth family $K$:
$$\sum_{d \in K} \frac{1}{\varphi(d)} = Z_y \cdot \Pr(D_y \in K).$$

So the missing lemma is exactly equivalent to
$$\sup_{\text{reachable } P} \Pr(D_y \in \mathcal{K}(P)) = o(1). \qquad (\star)$$

This is a clean probabilistic statement: prove that the natural Bernoulli $(1/p)_{p \le y}$-product measure on the squarefree $y$-smooth integers assigns asymptotically vanishing probability to any reachable closure $\mathcal{K}(P)$, uniformly over game-reachable Prolonger histories $P$.

## What is known about the gap

- **Medium-rank localization.** If every $d \in \mathcal{K}(P)$ has $\omega(d) = o(\log\log n)$, then $\sum 1/\varphi(d) = o(\log y)$ automatically. So any obstruction to $(\star)$ must live at medium-rank $y$-smooth supports with $\omega(d) \sim (\log\log n)/2$.
- **Static crude-divisor-incidence fails.** For $C_k = \{d : \omega(d) = k = \theta \log\log n\}$, the exponent gap $\theta(1 - \log\theta) - \mathsf{H}(\theta) > 0$ for $\theta \in (0, 1)$ means crude incidence counting cannot establish $(\star)$. Tighter analysis is needed.
- **Equivalent combinatorial form (defect-layer closure budget).** The same gap in its hypergraph-transversal form: on the auxiliary $h$-uniform hypergraph with vertices $=$ top-facet certificates $bA_{S \setminus \{p\}}$ and hyperedges $=$ $h$-sets of top facets for each rank-$h$ target, Shortener needs an online robust transversal of size $O(g(h) N_h/H)$ with $g(h) = o(h)$ against a Prolonger that steals the entire $h$-vertex boundary of one hyperedge per turn. The natural static Rödl cover has the right size $\sim N_h/H$ but does not survive boundary stealing.
- **Direct LYM transplant ruled out.** A literal Erdős-#1196-style weighted LYM inequality on divisibility antichains cannot bound $|A|$ below linear scale for this problem: the upper-half $(n/2, n]$ is itself an antichain of size $n/2$, insensitive to any weighted-LYM construction.

## What you are asked to attempt

Prove $(\star)$, refute it, or produce a partial result.

The Bernoulli framing makes probabilistic tools available that are not available for raw reciprocal sums:

1. **Second moment / Janson.** Bound $\Pr(D_y \in \mathcal{K}(P))$ via moment estimates on the number of Prolonger moves $p$ with $D_y \mid p$. Key question: how correlated are these events under the Bernoulli product measure?
2. **FKG correlation.** The Bernoulli product measure satisfies FKG on the squarefree divisor lattice. Events of the form $\{D_y \in \mathcal{K}(P)\}$ are decreasing in added primes (if $d \in \mathcal K$, then no superset of $d$'s prime support need be in $\mathcal K$; the complex is down-closed). Can FKG give uniform bounds over reachable $P$?
3. **Spread / sunflower methods on downward-closed sets.** $\mathcal{K}(P)$ is a downset in the Boolean lattice on primes $\le y$. Bernoulli mass of a downset of rank $\le k$ is bounded by $\binom{H}{k}$ (binary entropy) times $\max Z_y$; Reed embedding or Kahn-Saks might give tighter estimates.
4. **Direct game-theoretic bound.** Prolonger's sweep-move budget is $O(|C_y|) = o(n)$. The worst-case Prolonger can only produce a complex of total size $\le |C_y|$. If the reachable $\mathcal{K}(P)$ is forced by the game dynamics to have small Bernoulli mass (e.g., by being too "dispersed" across rank layers), $(\star)$ might follow automatically.
5. **Refutation route.** Construct a specific Prolonger sweep strategy producing a reachable $\mathcal{K}(P)$ with $\Pr(D_y \in \mathcal{K}(P)) \ge c > 0$. This would refute $(\star)$ and imply matching T2 upper bound fails — the actual rate is strictly between $n(\log\log n)^2/\log n$ and linear.

Partial results with specific constants, weakened hypotheses (e.g., the bound holds for any rank-$\le k$ closure but might fail at higher rank), or sharpenings of the medium-rank localization ($\omega(d)$ restricted further) are useful.

## Notation recap

- $n$ = game parameter, $y = \exp((\log n)^{1/2})$, $H = \sum_{p \le y} 1/p \sim \log\log n$.
- $D_y$ = random squarefree product, $p \le y$ included independently with prob $1/p$.
- $Z_y = \prod_{p \le y} p/(p-1) \sim e^\gamma \log y$.
- $\mathcal{K}(P)$ = down-closed $y$-smooth squarefree complex generated by Prolonger's sweep moves.
- "Reachable" $P$ means: any Prolonger history arising in some completion of the game where Shortener sweeps $C_y$ first.
