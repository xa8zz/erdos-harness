## The sharpened gap: defect-layer closure budget and a robust online transversal lemma

Fix a rank-$h$ target $t = bA_S$ with $|S| = h$, $A_S = \prod_{p \in S} p$, $b$ a large prime. A defect-$k$ certificate is a lateral $bA_D$ with $|S \setminus D| = k$. Let $H = \sum_{p \le y} 1/p \sim \log\log n$.

Raw sizes:

- Rank-$h$ targets: $N_h \asymp (n/\log n) H^h/h!$
- Defect-$k$ certificates: $V_{h,k} \asymp (n/\log n) H^{h-k}/(h-k)!$
- Targets killed per defect-$k$ certificate: $D_{h,k} \asymp H^k/k!$
- Certificates per target: $\binom{h}{k}$
- Rate identity: $V_{h,k}/\binom{h}{k} = N_h/D_{h,k}$ — no positive drift at any fixed defect layer; pair-shadow and static rank-$k$ arguments all fail for this reason.

Layer budget $B_{h,k} \asymp N_h k!/H^k$; total $B_h = \sum_k B_{h,k} \asymp (1+o(1)) N_h/H$, dominated by the $k=1$ top-facet layer. The **defect-layer closure budget**
$$\mathfrak B_h(R, C) := \sum_{k=1}^h \frac{|\partial_{h-k} R \setminus C_{h-k}|}{\binom{h}{k}}$$
is the full squarefree-closure analogue of pair-shadow mass, and has the right scale for a matching upper bound.

**Sublinear reduction (new).** A robust online transversal lemma
$$\text{rank-}h\ \text{residual score} \ll \frac{g(h)}{h} N_h, \quad g(h) = o(h)$$
implies $L(n) \ll n g(\log\log n)/\log\log n + n/(\log n)^{1-o(1)}$. Specifically:

- $g(h) = O(\log h)$ gives $L(n) \ll n \log\log\log n / \log\log n$ — solves the original problem.
- $g(h) = O(h^{1-\varepsilon})$ gives $L(n) \ll n/(\log\log n)^\varepsilon$ — also solves.
- $g(h) = O(1)$ gives $L(n) \ll n/\log\log n$.

**Online hypergraph game.** Define the $h$-uniform hypergraph $H^{(h)}$ with vertices $=$ top-facet certificates $bA_{S \setminus \{p\}}$ and hyperedges $=$ $h$-sets of top facets for each rank-$h$ target. On $H^{(h)}$:

- Shortener claims one vertex per turn.
- Prolonger steals all $h$ vertices of one unhit hyperedge per turn.

Shortener's goal: build a transversal at size $O(g(h) N_h/H)$ for some $g(h) = o(h)$. Ordinary static Rödl covering has the right scale $\sim N_h/H$ but does NOT survive Prolonger's boundary stealing. Need a genuinely online construction.

The natural greedy argument stalls at the **fully closed residual set**
$$\operatorname{Cl}_h(R, C) := \{S \in R : \text{every proper certificate } D \subsetneq S \text{ lies in } C\},$$
which is immune to lower-divisor attacks. The missing inequality is essentially $|\operatorname{Cl}_h(R, C)| \le O(g(h) \mathfrak B_h(R, C) + \text{already scored})$, $g(h) = o(h)$.

## What has been ruled out recently

- **Bernoulli reformulation of Gap 2 refuted.** A parallel analysis showed that for the smooth-squarefree certificate $C_y = \{d \le n/2 : d \text{ squarefree}, P^+(d) \le y\}$ and the random product $D_y$ with $\Pr(D_y = d) = 1/(Z_y \varphi(d))$, one has $\sum_{d \in K} 1/\varphi(d) = Z_y \Pr(D_y \in K)$. Under the "reachable" definition that allows any sweep completion, Prolonger has a legal prefix forcing $\Pr(D_y \in \mathcal K(P)) \ge 1 - o(1)$: for each $k$-subset $F$ of primes $\le y$ with $k = \lceil \mu + \sqrt\mu\log\mu\rceil$, play $x_F = (\prod_F p) \cdot q_F \in (n/2, n]$ alternating with Shortener playing $s_F = \prod_{G_F} p$ for an injection $F \mapsto G_F$ into $(k+1)$-sets; $\mathcal K(P)$ then contains every squarefree $y$-smooth integer with $\omega \le k$, and Chebyshev on $W_y = \omega(D_y)$ gives $\Pr(1 \le W_y \le k) = 1 - o(1)$. The entire construction uses $n^{o(1)}$ Prolonger moves. So the naive "certificate + reachable-complex" framing is unachievable for $C_y$.

  This refutes one of the two natural framings of the gap. The transversal-lemma framing (above) is strictly stronger and remains live, because it is about an abstract hypergraph game, not about reachable Bernoulli mass on a smooth-squarefree complex.

- **Within-core lateral cleanup only logarithmically effective.** For one activated fiber, $t$ optimal prime-lateral attacks reduce the residual reciprocal mass only by $1/\log t$, not by constant factor per move. So $g(h) = O(1)$ via within-core cleanup alone likely impossible. Cross-core cleanup via shared low divisors remains open.

- **Dyadic-fiber positive-density route collapsed.** The single-fiber positive-density theorem is equivalent to the linear conjecture itself (cross-fiber laterals incomparable, multi-fiber value $\le \sum_b L_{\mathrm{upper}}(K_b)$).
